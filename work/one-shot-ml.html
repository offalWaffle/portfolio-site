ML: One-Shot Instrument Generation

ML project: generating a full 88-key playable instrument from a single recorded note — by translating pitch in a neural codec's latent space.

Overview

Turning a single piano note into a full, playable 88-key instrument that holds its timbre across the entire range. It's basically pitch shifter that follows the harmonic characteristics of the one-shot recordings of piano multi samples the model was trained against. The approach i chose was to freeze a pretrained neural audio codec (Descript's DAC) and train a small (~27M-parameter) adapter that translates a source latent to a target pitch under direct supervision. It trains end-to-end in under an hour on a single GPU and reaches a 0.323 validation loss.

Approach

I abandoned two harder architectures first — a VQ-VAE that baked pitch into the latent it was meant to transform, and an adversarial disentanglement scheme where the classifier kept beating the encoder — before the key reframe: I didn't need a pitch-invariant representation, just a pitch-translated output, which turned an unwinnable adversarial game into a supervised regression. The two remaining bugs lived upstream of the model, not in it: a loss floor fixed with cleaner data, and a decay-tail "buzz" fixed by changing what the loss measured. The throughline — the biggest wins came from reframing the problem and fixing the data, not from a bigger model.

Details

  • Physical modelling
  • CMA-ES
  • Perceptual loss
